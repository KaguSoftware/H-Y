# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Mobile gamers, creators, and heavy phone users whose devices throttle under sustained load (gaming, fast charging, camera work, navigation in hot climates). They land on the site to understand what the H&Y cooler is and why it works, then buy.

## Product Purpose

Marketing site for the H&Y Mobile Phone Cooler: a 145 g clamp-on thermoelectric cooler (semiconductor/Peltier element, copper heat sheet, BCT-coated heat sink, silent 7-blade fan, USB-C powered, automatic speed and temperature control, contact plate down to -6 °C). Source of truth for all product claims: `public/MOBILE PHONE COOLER.pdf`. The device clamps on silicone pads (67-88 mm wide phones); it is NOT magnetic.

Site structure: `/` explains the cooling technology; `/product` is the scroll-driven product showcase (CoolingSequenceHero) with the buy CTA.

## Brand Personality

Precise, physical, quietly confident. An engineering company showing its work, not a gadget brand shouting. Voice of a thermal spec sheet that learned to tell a story: heat is the antagonist, cold is engineered.

## Anti-references

- Gamer-RGB aesthetics (neon everything, aggressive angles, carbon-fiber textures).
- Generic SaaS landing grammar: icon-card grids, hero-metric stats, gradient text.
- Dropship-store product pages with stacked badges and countdown timers.

## Design Principles

- Show the physics: explain and diagram how heat actually moves; never hand-wave with marketing adjectives.
- One world: the dark "stage" established by the 3D hero carries across every page.
- Heat-to-cold is the narrative axis: ember (#ff4d1f) means problem, ice (#5fd4ff) means solution.
- Every claim traces to the spec PDF; no invented numbers.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast on the dark stage. All scroll/entrance motion honors prefers-reduced-motion. Never rely on the red/cyan temperature coding alone; always pair with text.
